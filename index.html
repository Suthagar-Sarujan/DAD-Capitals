    Frequently Asked Questions

    Find quick answers to the most common questions about our platform, accounts, and trading services.

    What is Forex?

    Forex, or foreign exchange, is the global market for buying and selling currencies. It’s the largest financial market in the world, with trillions traded daily. Traders aim to profit from changes in currency exchange rates.

    How can you take advantage of exchange rate changes?

    You can profit by buying a currency when its value is low and selling it when it rises.
    For example, if you buy euros with 500 dollars and the exchange rate improves, you might exchange back and get 510 dollars, earning a 10-dollar profit.

    Which currencies can you trade?

    With DAD Capitals, you can trade over 80 currency pairs in three main groups:
    Major: The most traded pairs including USD, like EUR/USD and GBP/USD.
    Cross pairs: Pairs without USD, such as GBP/JPY and JPY/CAD.
    Exotics: Pairs combining a major currency with an emerging one, like USD/ZAR and USD/HKD.

    What are Pending Orders in Forex Trade?

    A pending order is an order set to execute only when the price reaches a specific level. Traders use it to buy or sell at support or resistance without waiting. There are four types, including a Buy Limit, which is an order to buy at a lower price than the current market.

    When can you trade forex?

    You can trade forex 24 hours a day, five days a week. The market is divided into four sessions: Sydney, Tokyo, London, and New York. This allows for nearly continuous trading worldwide.

    What is Leverage?

    Forex trading offers high leverage, allowing you to control a large amount with a small investment. For example, with 100 to 1 leverage, you only need 100 dollars to trade 10,000 dollars. If your trade gains 100 dollars, that is a 100 percent return, but losses can be just as big, so managing risk is important.

    What is Margin?

    Margin is the amount of money your broker asks for as a deposit to open a leveraged trade. For example, to control \$100,000 with leverage, you might need to put up \$1,000 as margin. The smaller the margin required, the higher the leverage you can use.

    How does cryptocurrency trading work?

    Cryptocurrency trading lets you buy and sell digital assets like Bitcoin to profit from price changes. With DAD Capitals, you can trade crypto CFDs, meaning you don’t own the actual coins but can profit from rising or falling prices. Leverage allows bigger trades with less money, while tools like stop-loss help manage risk in this volatile market.
